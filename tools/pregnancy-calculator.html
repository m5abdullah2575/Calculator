Understanding Pregnancy Calculation

Pregnancy is typically calculated as lasting about 40 weeks (280 days) from the first day of your last menstrual period (LMP). This calculation method is used because it's often difficult to determine the exact date of conception.

Key Facts

  • • Full-term pregnancy: 37-42 weeks
  • • Average pregnancy: 40 weeks from LMP
  • • Only 4% of babies are born on their due date
  • • 90% are born within 2 weeks of due date
  • • Pregnancy is divided into 3 trimesters

Calculation Methods

  • • Last Menstrual Period (LMP) - Most common
  • • Ultrasound dating - Most accurate early in pregnancy
  • • Conception date - If known precisely
  • • IVF transfer date - For assisted pregnancies
  • • Due date - Working backwards

Pregnancy Trimesters

First Trimester

Weeks 1-12

  • • Embryo development begins
  • • Major organ formation
  • • Morning sickness common
  • • Fatigue and mood changes
  • • First prenatal appointments
  • • Higher risk of miscarriage

Second Trimester

Weeks 13-27

  • • Often called the "golden period"
  • • Morning sickness typically subsides
  • • Baby movements felt
  • • Anatomy scan performed
  • • Gender can be determined
  • • Energy levels improve

Third Trimester

Weeks 28-40+

  • • Rapid baby growth
  • • Increased discomfort
  • • Preparation for labor
  • • Regular prenatal visits
  • • Baby positioning for birth
  • • Birth plan finalization

Accuracy and Limitations

Most Accurate Methods

  • Early Ultrasound (6-12 weeks): Most accurate method, especially before 12 weeks
  • IVF Transfer Date: Precise timing known for assisted pregnancies
  • Known Conception: When ovulation timing is tracked precisely

Limitations

  • LMP Method: Assumes regular 28-day cycles and day 14 ovulation
  • Irregular Cycles: Less accurate for women with irregular periods
  • Late Ultrasounds: Accuracy decreases after 12 weeks

Important: Due dates are estimates. Your healthcare provider may adjust your due date based on ultrasound measurements and other factors. Always consult with your healthcare provider for personalized medical advice.

Important Pregnancy Milestones

Week 4-6

First prenatal visit, confirm pregnancy

Week 8-12

First trimester screening, nuchal translucency

Week 15-20

Anatomy scan, gender determination

Week 18-22

First baby movements felt (quickening)

Week 24-28

Glucose screening test, viability threshold

Week 32-36

Weekly appointments, baby positioning
