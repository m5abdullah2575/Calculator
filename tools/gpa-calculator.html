How GPA is Calculated

Grade Point Average (GPA) is calculated by dividing the total quality points earned by the total credit hours attempted. Quality points are calculated by multiplying the grade point value by the number of credit hours for each course.

GPA Formula

GPA = Total Quality Points ÷ Total Credit Hours

Example Calculation

Course Credits Grade Points
Math 4 A (4.0) 16.0
English 3 B+ (3.3) 9.9
History 3 A- (3.7) 11.1
Total 10 - 37.0
GPA = 37.0 ÷ 10 = 3.70

Key Points

  • Higher credit courses have more impact on GPA
  • Grade points vary by institution
  • Some schools don't count + grades (A+)
  • Pass/No Pass courses may not count
  • Repeated courses may have special rules

Tips for Improving Your GPA

Academic Strategies

  • Attend All Classes: Regular attendance helps you stay engaged and catch important information
  • Take Good Notes: Organize and review notes regularly to reinforce learning
  • Form Study Groups: Collaborative learning can improve understanding
  • Use Office Hours: Build relationships with professors and get help when needed

Time Management

  • Create a Schedule: Plan study time for each course based on difficulty and credits
  • Set Priorities: Focus more time on challenging courses and major requirements
  • Avoid Procrastination: Break large assignments into smaller, manageable tasks
  • Review Regularly: Consistent review is more effective than cramming

Pro Tip: Course Load Management

Consider your course load carefully. It's better to take fewer courses and earn higher grades than to overload yourself and struggle. A strategic approach to course selection can significantly impact your GPA over time.
